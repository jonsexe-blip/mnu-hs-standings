Methodology Notes & Algorithm Transparency

What the standings.pdf tells us

  • Algorithm: MN-RRI is a modified version of USA Ultimate's RRI, itself derived from KRACH (Bradley-Terry model)
  • Formula: RRI = ln(KRACH), producing a linear-like scale from an exponential KRACH base
  • Score margins matter: A 15-1 win counts more than a 8-7 win. Winners earn score/(score_A+score_B), losers earn the complement
  • Chain of opponents: RRI requires a connected path of results between any two teams being compared. Disconnected teams cannot be meaningfully compared
  • Previous year supplement: Early in the season, prior-year games are used for scheduling. For State seeding, only current-year games count
  • ~3 rounds needed: RRI is unreliable until enough inter-team connections form

Shortcomings & Unknowns

  • Partial tournament coverage: 333 games computed here cover regular-season league play plus Matoska Classic, Spring Jamboree, and Hopkins Hustle D1 bracket. Hopkins Hustle pool play and D2/D3 results are not yet available — this explains remaining discrepancies
  • Previous-year weighting: MNU may include prior-year games in current standings. Those games are not available here
  • Undefeated edge cases: Benilde (7-0) and St Paul Academy (6-0) theoretically produce infinite KRACH. MNU handles this (likely via smoothing), method undisclosed. This computation uses 0.01 minimum win value as a floor
  • Out-of-state opponents: 4 Wisconsin teams (Matoska Classic) are included in KRACH math for strength-of-schedule accuracy but do not appear in MN rankings. Games vs WI teams count for MN team KRACH but not MNU official records
  • Small samples: Teams with few games in this dataset (Providence: 4 games, Hopkins JV: 5, Eagan JV: 5) have less stable computed RRI values
  • Score-weighted wins: My computation uses point differential per game as the RRI weight. MNU's exact formula may differ slightly
  • Published scale: MNU's published logarithmic scale may be shifted/normalized differently than this computation

How Are MNU Standings Determined?

Based on MNU Staff and High School Committee documentation (standings.pdf).

MNU uses MN-RRI, a modified version of USA Ultimate's RRI — the same system that determines club and college nationals bids. It has been refined over 5+ years for the unique challenges of high school ultimate.

KRACH — The Foundation

KRACH (Ken's Rating for American College Hockey) applies the Bradley-Terry model to produce dimensionless numbers predicting the odds for any two teams in a head-to-head match. If A has KRACH = 200 and B has KRACH = 100, A has 2-to-1 odds — a 67% win probability.

The Bradley-Terry maximum-likelihood estimate is solved iteratively:

KRACH_i = W_i / Σ_j [ games(i,j) / (KRACH_i + KRACH_j) ]

Where W_i is team i's total win value (score-weighted). This self-referential equation is iterated until convergence (this computation needed 900 iterations to reach 10⁻⁹ precision).

RRI — Adapted for Ultimate

  • Score margins: Each game, the winner earns score_winner / (score_winner + score_loser). A 15-1 blowout gives the winner 0.938; a 8-7 squeaker gives only 0.533.
  • Logarithmic scale: RRI = ln(KRACH). This compresses the enormous KRACH range into a manageable scale while preserving all relative information.
RRI = ln(KRACH)

A difference of 1.0 RRI → e ≈ 2.72× KRACH ratio → 73% win probability for the higher team. A difference of 0.2 → 55% probability. A difference of 0 → exactly 50/50.

Strength of Schedule

The Bradley-Terry model's key property: it propagates strength through chains of results. If A beat B who beat C, then A's KRACH is implicitly anchored to C's, even if they never played. This is exactly strength-of-schedule accounting — no manual adjustment needed.

How MNU Applies It

  • Only sanctioned tournaments between League teams count
  • Early in the season, prior-year games supplement current data for scheduling
  • For the State Championship, only current-year games are used
  • MNU publishes a logarithmic scale, not raw KRACH, because raw values span near-0 to hundreds of millions
  • Reliable RRI requires approximately 3 rounds of games for sufficient chain connectivity

Special Cases

  • Undefeated teams (Benilde 7-0, St Paul Academy 6-0): Pure KRACH → ∞. MNU handles this; method undisclosed. This computation adds a minimum win floor (0.01) to stabilize.
  • Winless teams: KRACH → 0 / RRI → −∞. Same floor approach.
  • Disconnected teams: If no result chain connects two teams, their relative RRI is undefined.
Why it matters: As MNU approaches field capacity at State, RRI provides a transparent, objective, bias-free selection method — grounded entirely in game results, not committee opinion.
